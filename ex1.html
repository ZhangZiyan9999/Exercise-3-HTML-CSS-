Useful Websites for Study

Academic Research and Journals

  • Google Scholar - A freely accessible web search engine that indexes the full text or metadata of scholarly literature across various formats and disciplines.
  • JSTOR - A digital library for academic journals, books, and primary sources.
  • ScienceDirect - A leading full-text scientific database offering journal articles and book chapters from more than 2,500 peer-reviewed journals and more than 11,000 books.

Programming and Software Engineering

  • Stack Overflow - A question and answer site for professional and enthusiast programmers.
  • GitHub - A platform for version control and collaboration. It lets you and others work together on projects from anywhere.
  • W3Schools - A web developer's site, with tutorials and references on web development languages such as HTML, CSS, JavaScript, PHP, SQL, and Bootstrap.

Virtual Reality and Haptics

  • Virtual Reality Society - An organization dedicated to the development and promotion of virtual reality and augmented reality.
  • Road to VR - The latest news and insights on virtual reality and augmented reality technology.
  • Haptics Industry Forum - A forum dedicated to advancing the use and adoption of haptic feedback technology.

Language Learning and Exchange Programs

  • Duolingo - A popular language-learning platform that uses gamification to make learning languages fun and effective.
  • Coursera - Offers online courses from top universities and companies. It includes language courses and programs.
  • Erasmus+ - The EU's program to support education, training, youth, and sport in Europe, offering opportunities for students to study abroad.
